My mother's spacial dhokla

About Dhokla

Dhokla is a vegetarian culinary dish that is native to the Indian state of Gujarat and parts of adjacent states. Although a Gujarati delicacy., it is also commonly served in other parts of India. It is made with a fermented batter derived from legumes like Chickpea, Pigeon pea and Urad and rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup gram flour
  • 1 tbsp citric acid
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • to taste salt
  • A pinch of turmeric
  • Water(to make batter)
  • 1 tbsp fruit salt/ baking powder
  • For the Tadka:
    •
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tbsp mustard seeds
  • 1 dried red chilli
  • 7-8 curry leaves

Steps

  1. In a bowl mix gram flour, citric acid, salt, sugar and turmeric. Add water and make it into a smooth batter with medium thick consistency.
  2. In a glass add the fruit salt or baking powder. Add water to it and pour this into the dhokla mixture.
  3. Grease the steaming tin with 2 drops of oil and pour the mixture into it. Steam for about 15-20 minutes or till cooked.
  4. In a pan, add oil, mustard seeds, curry leaves and red chillies. Let it splutter.
  5. Pour the tadka over the prepared dhokla.
  6. Cut into pieces and serve.
